Job Fraud

Job fraud refers to deceptive practices that target job seekers, often leading them to believe they are applying for legitimate employment opportunities when, in reality, the job does not exist or is not as described. Job fraud can take various forms, such as fake job postings, work-from-home scams, pyramid schemes, and phishing scams.

Matrimonial Fraud

Matrimonial fraud involves deceitful practices in the context of marriage or romantic relationships. This type of fraud can occur when an individual misrepresents themselves or their intentions to a partner, often for financial gain or other personal advantages.

Financial Fraud

Financial fraud encompasses a wide range of deceptive practices that aim to unlawfully acquire money or assets through dishonest means. This type of fraud can occur in various forms, including securities fraud, accounting fraud, insurance fraud, identity theft, credit card fraud, and investment scams.

Cyber hygiene

Cyber hygiene refers to the practices and precautions individuals and organizations should take to maintain their digital health and security. It involves adopting good habits to protect against cyber threats and keep personal and sensitive information safe.
